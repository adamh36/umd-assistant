You’ll develop the skills to transform data into insight and apply it in real-world business contexts.The program emphasizes applied, project-based learning, giving you opportunities to work on real business problems using data and analytics tools.Graduates of the program qualify to earn the Associate Certified Analytics Professional (aCAP) credential.Designed for both full-time and part-time students, the program offers evening classes on campus and several online course options, allowing you to earn your degree on your schedule.Want to jump start your degree? If you’re a current UM-Dearborn undergraduate student and you’ve been admitted to the master’s program, you can take up to six graduate credits during your final undergraduate semester.Where a Master’s in Business Analytics Will Take YouGraduates are prepared for roles in business intelligence and analytics, consulting and operations analysis, and data-informed decision support across industries. These roles span functions such as finance, marketing, operations, and supply chain, allowing you to apply analytics across a wide range of business contexts.With a foundation in analytics and intelligent systems, you’ll be equipped to help organizations turn data into insight and support better decision-making across industries such as finance, healthcare, manufacturing, and technology.If you are interested in a more specialized path, the College of Business also offers focused graduate programs in financial analytics and marketing analytics.The STEM designation provides international students with an extended Optional Practical Training (OPT) period, enhancing opportunities for valuable U.S. work experience.Our career support services, global alumni network, and expansive networking opportunities will help you launch and grow your career. Program Details What You’ll Study The MS in Business Analytics is evolving to incorporate an intelligent systems approach that reflects the growing role of data, artificial intelligence, and enterprise systems in modern organizations.The curriculum integrates analytics, programming, statistical modeling, and information systems to help you understand how intelligent systems are designed, implemented, and used in organizations.Students who are admitted to the MS in Business Analytics in the 2026-27 academic year will begin following this new curriculum:Business Analytics and Intelligent Systems Foundations (12 credits)BA 520 - AI Fundamentals for Business (3 credits)BA 530 - Programming and Data Structures with Python (3 credits)DS 520 - Applied Statistical Modeling (3 credits)ISM 525 - Fundamentals of Information Systems (3 credits)Analytics, Intelligent Systems, and Managerial Core (12 credits) Four graduate courses from any of the following:BA 605 - Managerial Decision MakingAny Decision Sciences courses, such as:DS 570 - Prescriptive Business AnalyticsDS 630 - Applied Forecasting with PythonDS 633 - Machine Learning for Business IntelligenceAny Information Systems Management courses, such as:ISM 575 - Information and Database ManagementISM 580 - AI Application DevelopmentISM 650 - Systems Development and Data QualityPersonalized Pathway (6 credits)Complete any two College of Business graduate courses (3 credits each) Legacy Program Requirements Academic Pathway Program (CAP) The CAP Academic Pathway Program is an ideal opportunity for students looking to differentiate themselves early in their analytics career. This program connects your academic training in the MS in Business Analytics program with an industry-recognized credential and prepares you for future Certified Analytics Professional (CAP) certification.Graduates can earn the credential by joining INFORMS, completing a data ethics course, and following a simple certification process. This program helps launch careers in analytics while providing access to professional development and resources through INFORMS. Practical Skills You’ll Gain Graduates of the MS in Business Analytics program develop a well-rounded skill set that combines analytics, programming, and an understanding of how data and systems support decision-making in modern organizations.Through required and elective coursework, you can develop skills such as:Analyzing data to identify patterns, trends, and opportunitiesDeveloping analytical models to support business decision-makingApplying statistical and forecasting techniques to real-world problemsWorking with data and the systems that collect, manage, and support itUnderstanding how intelligent systems use data to support decision-makingCommunicating findings clearly to technical and non-technical audiencesApplying analytics across business functions such as finance, marketing, operations, and supply chain Transfer Credit and Course Exemptions Previous coursework deemed substantially similar to ISM 525, or an undergraduate degree in Information Systems Management, may qualify to exempt students from ISM 525. Exempt courses must be replaced with other elective courses in the degree program.In addition, up to 9 transfer credits for previous equivalent graduate coursework can be applied to the degree if those credits have not been counted toward a degree.Exemptions and transfer credit are granted at the discretion of the program faculty. Admission Requirements This program welcomes students from all undergraduate majors and levels of work experience. We conduct a holistic review of each application, focusing on strong academic performance, quantitative skills, and a clear statement of purpose. Admission is selective.
