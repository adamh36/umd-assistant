Your courses in the nine-credit program will introduce you to the foundations, emerging practices, and real-world applications of data analytics and machine learning for power systems.You can take all courses for the certificate in person, online, or a combination of both. We schedule them in the late afternoon and evening so they’re convenient for part-time students who work. You can apply credits earned for the certificate toward graduate degrees offered by UM-Dearborn’s Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering.Pathway to a Master's DegreePlan ahead by selecting certificate courses that overlap with graduate courses required for a related master's program. Doing so allows you to earn your certificate while simultaneously making progress toward your master's degree. Meet with an advisor to learn more! Program Details What You’ll Study The required core course, Data Analytics and Machine Learning for Power Systems, is an introduction to the subject. It covers a broad range of topics, including data collection, data processing, and data mining for electrical power systems. You’ll get hands-on experience through computer-based simulation projects.To complete the three-course certificate, you select two courses from three options:Data MiningIntelligent SystemsPattern Recognition and Neural Networks Explore Program Requirements Ready to Apply?
