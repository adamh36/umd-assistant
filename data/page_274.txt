This certificate program deepens your understanding of the field and provides a useful credential. It combines theoretical knowledge with practical application, preparing you for a world that’s increasingly reliant on the interplay between materials, manufacturing and design.You can apply the nine credits you earn for the certificate to an undergraduate mechanical engineering degree program at the University of Michigan-Dearborn. All classes are offered on campus, and some are occasionally offered online. Program Details What You’ll Study The choice is yours. To earn the certificate, you complete three courses from seven options. They cover subjects like manufacturing design, manufacturing processes, nanoscience and nanotechnology, biomaterial tissue engineering, and digital manufacturing, among others.The courses emphasize practice-based learning. You’ll not only learn the theoretical aspects of manufacturing engineering, but also apply these concepts in real-world scenarios.
