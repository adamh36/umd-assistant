It’s available to undergraduate students in the College of Arts, Sciences, and Letters; the College of Business; and the College of Education, Health, and Human Services. The minor or integrative studies concentration consists of four upper-level courses you choose from a dozen options in the disciplines of economics, political science, and public administration and public policy. You can choose from courses in public administration, fundraising, network collaboration, strategic management for public administration, program evaluation, and revitalizing cities, among other subjects.
