Breadcrumb Home Events EIC's 25th Anniversary EIC's 25th Anniversary For: Everyone Saturday, May 16, 2026 9:00 am - 6:30 pm Environmental Interpretive Center, Main Lobby, Various spaces Outdoor in Environmental Study Area (map) Join UM-Dearborn for a 25th-anniversary celebration of the Environmental Interpretive Center (EIC), honoring its history as a "living classroom" while supporting the 25 for 25 Campaign, which aims to raise $25,000 for the center’s future.This is an open-house event featuring a cultural presentation with Hadassah GreenSky, an EIC history talk by Rick Simek, and a bird song-and-music program with guest Andy Jarema. Enjoy guided outdoor walks and hands-on activities, including birding, a pond program, and more.Event Itinerary May 16th:Bird Walk 9-10:30 AM (separate Google form signup)*Welcome/Cultural Performance w/Hadassah GreenSky 11:00 AMCake Cutting 12:30 PM***Concurrent breakout activities 1:00 PM **Stewardship Saturday 2:30-4:30 PM**Evening stroll and campfire 5- 6:30 PM *RSVP required, limited capacity.**Unique Google Form signup***Concurrent breakout activities (see below)Fungi Fundamentals Walk: Join mushroom enthusiast Mike Solomon, one of EIC’s Certified Interpretive Guides, for a guided walk along the Interpretive Trails. Together, you'll discover various fungi and learn about their vital role in our local ecosystem.EIC History Talk: Rick Simek is the current EIC Habitat Manager and Interpretive Naturalist and has been at the EIC for more than three decades. Listen to Rick’s talk entitled “Lessons in the Leaves: From Estate to Living Laboratory”.Three Sisters Garden Walk and Talk: Shiloh Maples, an Anishinaabe seedkeeper and organizer, will be leading a walk from the EIC to the Three Sisters Garden and medicine garden. Join us as we learn about Indigenous foods and medicines.Nature Song and Music: Andy Jarema is a local music teacher/composer who incorporates natural sound into his educational and artistic practice. Join him for a session that will open your ears to the magical music of the natural sound world, including "guess the natural sound" quizzes and real-time music creation from environmental sounds.Q&A w/ Hadassah Greensky: Participate in an informal conversation with Hadassah Greensky about her performance, activism, and ongoing cultural work. Self-guided Interpretive Walk: Take a walk from the EIC building to Fair Lane Lake, then follow the Historic Loop Trail. Interact with EIC Student Naturalists for binocular viewing along the lake and drop into an informal pond experience in Clara Ford’s old Rose Garden. All ages and abilities are welcome!Seasonal Scavenger Hunts: Pick up a scavenger-hunt checklist at the front desk and explore the natural area! Observe nature at your own pace, then return to the EIC with your card and show it to an EIC Staff member to pick up a “prize”.Want to support the EIC, but can’t make the May 16 event? Consider volunteering or donating to celebrate the EIC’s 25 years.
