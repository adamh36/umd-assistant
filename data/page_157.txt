Optimize with Systems Thinking.Earn a dual degree that combines a future-ready MBA with advanced expertise in industrial and systems engineering. The University of Michigan-Dearborn’s MBA/MSE in Industrial and Systems Engineering prepares you to lead strategically while improving the performance of complex systems involving people, data, and technology.You’ll build a strategic mindset through core MBA courses in Business Analytics, AI for Business, Stakeholder-Centric Management, Global Business, and Decision-Making, laying the foundation for business electives in key functional areas. At the same time, the MSE-ISE curriculum equips you to analyze and optimize large-scale systems, enhancing performance across sectors like manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare. You’ll gain the skills to drive efficiency, systems thinking, and smart design in diverse operational environments.With flexible options for fully online, evening, or hybrid study, you can complete the program on a schedule that works for you. Most students attend part time and can complete the program in three years--or less if you qualify to waive MBA foundation courses.Want to jump start your degrees? If you’re a current UM-Dearborn undergraduate student and you’ve been admitted to the master’s program, you can take up to six graduate credits during your final undergraduate semester.Where a Dual MBA/MS in Industrial and Systems Engineering Will Take YouThis dual degree prepares you to lead at the intersection of engineering and management--making you a “dual threat” in the job market. With expertise in both strategic decision-making and complex systems optimization, you’ll be equipped for roles in manufacturing, logistics, healthcare, tech, and beyond. The program’s broad scope gives you the flexibility to shape your own career path while standing out in fields that value both technical and business acumen. With career services, alumni connections, and real-world relevance, you’ll be ready to move forward with confidence. Program Details What You'll Study The MBA/MSE in Industrial and Systems Engineering combines structured business leadership training with flexible, technically focused engineering coursework—giving you the tools to drive strategic decisions and operational improvements across complex organizations.On the MBA side, you’ll build foundational knowledge across key business functions and develop advanced skills in analytics, global management, stakeholder-centered leadership, and AI applications. A capstone project brings together everything you’ve learned, preparing you to solve modern business challenges from a top-level, cross-functional perspective.The MSE-ISE curriculum offers broad flexibility, allowing you to shape your studies around your career goals. You'll complete three required courses—Human Factors and Ergonomics, Design and Analysis of Experiments, and Production and Operations Engineering I—and choose the remainder from five concentration areas:Human Factors and ErgonomicsOperations Research and Management ScienceQuality Systems and ManufacturingInformation SystemsProgram Management and Product DevelopmentYou can specialize in one area or mix courses across multiple concentrations, building a custom learning experience that aligns with your interests and ambitions.The entire program includes 19-22 courses, or 57-66 credits, depending on whether you’ve previously taken courses that qualify you to waive some MBA foundations courses. Explore Program Requirements Practical Skills You’ll Gain This dual degree equips you with a powerful blend of strategic leadership and systems engineering skills. You’ll learn to lead teams, manage operations, and make data-informed decisions through real-world projects, case studies, and a strategy capstone. Courses in analytics, AI, and stakeholder-focused leadership will strengthen your ability to communicate and drive change across organizations.On the engineering side, you’ll gain hands-on experience improving complex systems using tools like optimization, human factors design, Six Sigma, and enterprise information systems. Whether managing global programs or streamlining operations, you’ll graduate ready to lead with both strategic insight and technical precision. Online Option Our online programs meet the same course requirements and admission standards as campus-based programs, and your diploma and transcript will be identical. Whether you complete your degree online, on campus, or in a hybrid format, it’s the same degree. Online students are welcome to enroll in campus-based courses and are encouraged to take advantage of campus resources and in-person events, like professional organizations and study-abroad opportunities.Asynchronous Virtual ClassroomCollege of Business online courses follow the regular semester schedule and are mostly delivered asynchronously. This means your professors will set deadlines, but you’ll have flexibility in when you complete assignments, tests, and activities. Unless specified, there’s no need to log in at a specific time, allowing you to participate from anywhere with internet access! To keep you engaged, our online courses often include group discussions, videos, projects, and quizzes or exams.Personalized Attention and SupportAs an online graduate student, you’ll have direct access to your professors, just as if you were on campus. You’ll also receive priority registration for online courses each semester. For academic advising, call us at 313-593-4776 to schedule a phone appointment during daytime or early evening hours, or email quick questions to umd-cobgradadvisor@umich.edu. While not required, we encourage online students to take advantage of campus resources and events, including professional organizations and study-abroad opportunities. Additionally, you’ll have full access to digital resources like the UM-Dearborn library. Course Waivers and Transfer Credit Students may waive ACC 505, BE 530, BPS 516, FIN 531, ISM / MIS 525, MKT 515, and OB 510 if they have equivalent courses in an AACSB business program completed within the previous 10 years and have earned at least a 3.2 post-60 GPA (that is, your GPA in courses taken after your first 60 undergraduate credit hours). Students who do not meet these criteria may request to have their courses evaluated for waiver credit at the time of admission. Students must have earned a B or better in equivalent courses as a part of a degree program completed within the previous 10 years.Regardless of waiver and exemption credits granted, students must earn at least 57 credits in the dual-degree program.In addition, up to 9 transfer credits for previous equivalent graduate coursework can be applied to the degree if those credits have not been counted toward a degree.Waivers and transfer credit are granted at the discretion of the program faculty. Admission Requirements This program welcomes students from all undergraduate majors and levels of work experience.
