The program also helps you move toward CPA licensure and prepares you to navigate the increasing overlap between financial strategy and regulatory compliance. Whether your goal is to lead in industry, public practice, or financial services, this powerful combination of credentials will position you to succeed in a competitive, data-driven world. Program Details What You'll Study The dual MS in Accounting/MS in Finance provides a comprehensive curriculum that blends core accounting and finance competencies with in-demand data and analytics skills.You’ll build a solid foundation in accounting, economics, business statistics, and finance—followed by advanced coursework in areas like auditing, taxation, financial reporting, corporate finance, investment management, and financial statement analysis. Analytics is integrated throughout the curriculum, with courses in business analytics and specialized applications in both audit and finance.Electives let you tailor your experience, with options such as AI fundamentals, Python programming, information systems auditing, fintech, fixed income securities, algorithmic finance, and more.The program requires 17-18 courses, or 51-54 credits, depending on whether you qualify to waive business economics or introductory finance. Explore Program Requirements Practical Skills You'll Gain You’ll build advanced skills in financial analysis, risk management, and strategic decision-making. You’ll learn to interpret financial statements, apply valuation techniques, and use analytics tools to visualize data and model outcomes. Courses emphasize real-world applications, including hands-on use of the Bloomberg Finance Lab and the opportunity to earn Bloomberg Market Concepts certification. You’ll also gain experience with Python programming, AI fundamentals, and data-driven auditing and tax strategies—plus the ability to assess organizational risk, evaluate internal controls, and clearly communicate insights to diverse stakeholders. Discounts on CPA Exam Preparation To support your success on the CPA exams, the College of Business partners with UWorld to provide access to online CPA preparation courses at a significant discount -- up to 50% off the prices offered to others.Students must meet specific accounting and general business course requirements in order to qualify for the CPA examination in Michigan. See the National Association of State Boards of Accountancy for the official policies. Admission Requirements This program welcomes students from all undergraduate majors and levels of work experience. We conduct a holistic review of each application, focusing on strong academic performance, quantitative skills, and a clear statement of purpose. Admission is selective.
