This graduate certificate program at the University of Michigan-Dearborn focuses on protecting electrical systems from faults, communication models for monitoring and controlling electrical systems, and cybersecurity for the smart grid. The program provides an excellent opportunity for young professionals who want to enhance their skills, as well as for experienced professionals who want to change careers.You can take all courses for the nine-credit certificate in person, online, or a combination of both. We schedule them in the late afternoon and evening so they’re convenient for part-time students who work. You can apply credits earned for the certificate toward graduate degrees offered by UM-Dearborn’s Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering.Pathway to a Master's DegreePlan ahead by selecting certificate courses that overlap with graduate courses required for a related master's program. Doing so allows you to earn your certificate while simultaneously making progress toward your master's degree. Meet with an advisor to learn more! Program Details What You’ll Study The nine-credit certificate consists of three courses:Grid Communication and SystemGrid ProtectionAdvanced Grid ProtectionThese courses explore a wide range of topics: legacy protocols and modern approaches, cyber-physical systems security for power grids, symmetrical component calculations and use, protection coordination, central station and distributed generator protection, fault studies, arc-flash calculations, and much more. You’ll experience the real world of electrical system protections through our practice-based learning approach, which combines theory and practical skills throughout the curriculum.
