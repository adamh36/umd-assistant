The certificate program offers an ideal opportunity for both young professionals looking to enhance their skills and experienced professionals seeking a career shift.You can take all courses for the nine-credit certificate in person, online, or a combination of both. We schedule them in the late afternoon and evening so they’re convenient for part-time students who work. You can apply credits earned for the certificate toward graduate degrees offered by UM-Dearborn’s Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering.Pathway to a Master's DegreePlan ahead by selecting certificate courses that overlap with graduate courses required for a related master's program. Doing so allows you to earn your certificate while simultaneously making progress toward your master's degree. Meet with an advisor to learn more! Program Details What You’ll Study The three courses in the certificate program cover topics such as transformers, DC and AC machines and drives, power electronics devices, DC-DC and DC-AC power conversions, risk-benefit analysis, cost-benefit analysis, and reliability analysis, among others. Our practice-based learning approach allows you to actively apply the theoretical knowledge gained during your studies. Explore Program Requirements Ready to Apply?
