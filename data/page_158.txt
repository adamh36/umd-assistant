You’ll be prepared for roles that combine strategic leadership with supply chain expertise, including positions focused on circular economy innovation, ESG compliance, and analytics-driven operations.Our career support services, global alumni network, and expansive networking opportunities will help you land your dream job. The STEM designation in the MS in Supply Chain Management provides international students with an extended Optional Practical Training (OPT) period, enhancing opportunities to gain valuable U.S. work experience after graduation. Program Details What You’ll Study The dual MBA/MS in Supply Chain Management curriculum blends core business knowledge with advanced supply chain expertise and a strong focus on analytics and sustainability. You’ll build strategic leadership skills through courses in finance, marketing, business analytics, AI, and global management. You'll also explore supply chain topics like logistics, procurement, and ESG-driven decision-making. Electives in both programs let you tailor your experience to your goals, and a capstone course prepares you to lead complex initiatives in global business and operations.The program consists of 19-25 courses, or 57-75 credits, depending on whether you’ve previously taken courses that qualify you to waive the MBA Foundation courses. Explore Program Requirements Practical Skills You’ll Gain In this dual-degree program, you’ll build the strategic, analytical, and leadership capabilities to drive innovation and sustainability across complex business operations. Through case studies, consulting-style projects, and a hands-on capstone, you’ll sharpen your ability to lead teams, make data-informed decisions, and communicate with impact.You’ll also gain the tools to optimize supply chains for both efficiency and environmental responsibility--applying analytics, Lean and Six Sigma methods, and emerging technologies to reduce carbon footprints and implement ethical sourcing strategies. The result is a versatile, practice-based skill set that prepares you to lead with purpose in a data-driven, globally connected world. Course Waivers, Exemptions and Transfer Credit Students may waive ACC 505, BE 530, BPS 516, FIN 531, ISM 525, MKT 515, or OB 510 if they have equivalent courses in an AACSB business program completed within the previous 10 years and have earned at least a 3.2 post-60 GPA (that is, your GPA in courses taken after your first 60 undergraduate credit hours). Students who do not meet these criteria may request to have their courses evaluated for waiver credit at the time of admission. Students must have earned a B or better in equivalent courses as a part of a degree program completed within the previous 10 years.Previous coursework deemed substantially similar to DS 520 or OM 521 may qualify to exempt students from those courses. Exempt courses must be replaced with other MS-Supply Chain Management Elective Courses.Regardless of waiver and exemption credits granted, students must earn at least 57 credits in the dual-degree program. In addition, up to 9 previous transfer credits for equivalent graduate coursework can be applied to the degree if those credits have not previously been counted toward a degree.Exemptions, waivers and transfer credit are granted at the discretion of the program faculty. Admission Requirements This program welcomes students from all undergraduate majors and levels of work experience. We conduct a holistic review of each application, focusing on strong academic performance, quantitative skills, and a clear statement of purpose. Admission is selective.
